# Keyword Strategy — Taiwan · Japan · Europe/US

The same article ranks for *different* queries in each market, because each
market searches with different intent and vocabulary. Don't translate
keywords — research them per language. Below are the themes and seed terms to
build on (validate volumes in Search Console, Google Trends, and your
autocomplete in each market).

## How the three markets differ

| | 🇹🇼 Taiwan (zh-TW) | 🇯🇵 Japan (ja) | 🇪🇺🇺🇸 Europe/US (en) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Search engine** | Google (dominant) | Google (dominant; Yahoo! Japan also runs on Google) | Google; Bing notable in US/EU |
| **Intent slant** | "怎麼用 Python 做…" tutorial + concept blends | high-quality長文 explainers; careful, study-oriented | concept + "explained", comparison, "with code" |
| **Vocabulary note** | mixes English tech terms (Python, AI) with 繁中 concepts | uses katakana for tech (パイソン/Python), kanji for concepts | precise topic nouns; "vs" comparisons perform well |
| **Device** | mobile-first | mobile-first; long dwell time | desktop + mobile mixed |

## Core brand & topic positioning (all markets)

You sit at the intersection of **history/philosophy × data science/Python**.
That niche is your moat — few competitors combine them. Own these umbrella
ideas:

- en: *history through data*, *philosophy with Python*, *data storytelling*,
  *computational history*, *quantum and philosophy*
- zh-TW: *用程式碼讀歷史*、*Python 數據分析 歷史*、*數據看哲學*、*量子力學 哲學*
- ja: *データで読む歴史*、*Pythonで歴史分析*、*量子力学 哲学*、*データサイエンス 教養*

## Title/description formula by market

- **en:** `{Topic}, Explained / vs {Topic}` + a payoff ("with Python",
  "a network analysis"). Comparisons and "vs" earn clicks.
- **zh-TW:** lead with the curiosity hook (`為什麼…？`) + method (`用 Python`).
  Mixing the English term (Python, AI) is natural and searched.
- **ja:** lead with the noun phrase, follow with `Pythonで解析/読み解く`. Avoid
  clickbait tone; Japanese readers reward substance and clarity.

## Content-gap & cluster ideas (to win more queries)

1. **Pillar pages** per series, per language (e.g. "用 Python 看文藝復興 完整指南"
   / "Renaissance through data: the complete guide") that link to every article
   — strong for topical authority and sitelinks.
2. **"How I built this" / methodology posts** (NetworkX, Bass model, matplotlib)
   capture the *tutorial* intent that's huge in TW & JP, then funnel to the
   history articles.
3. **Cross-market piece:** the Japan vs Taiwan housing article (S6-04) is a
   natural magnet for *both* JP and TW search — promote it in both.
4. **Glossary/FAQ entries** for terms like 中介中心性 / betweenness centrality /
   媒介中心性 — captures definitional and AI-overview queries.
5. **Update cadence:** refresh top articles yearly and bump `dateModified`.

## Validation loop

1. Publish with the `meta-tags.md` titles.
2. After 2–4 weeks, open Search Console → Performance → filter by page & country.
3. Find queries where you rank 5–15 (page 1–2 edge) and where CTR is low.
4. Fold those exact query phrases into the title/description/H2s, re-publish,
   bump `dateModified`. Repeat.
